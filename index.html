The Room

Low light, red leather,
and a little romance

Push through the door and the century falls away. Candles throw amber light across the booths, ice cracks behind the bar, and somewhere a record is playing just loud enough. This is a supper club the way they used to make them — a place to linger, to order the second martini, to celebrate the ordinary Tuesday.

We built Cloverdale for the long dinner: the kind that starts with oysters and ends with someone insisting on one more round. Come hungry, come dressed for it, and stay a while.
